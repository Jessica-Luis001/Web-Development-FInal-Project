Residence Hall Association

In an effort to maintain and continuously improve the quality of life on campus, advocates for residents of the Drew campus to the Office of Residential Life and to the greater Drew community; regularly reviews the condition and overall living experience of the Residence Halls; serves as a liaison between Drew University residents and the National Association of College and University Residence Halls; hosts annual events and programs designed to promote community and create an enjoyable atmosphere at Drew University.

Position Name

      

Resident Housing Association (RHA) is, in simple terms, a student-based organization that focuses on the issues and concerns pertaining to the residence halls and serves to improve the quality of life on
campus.Our main focuses pertain to programming campus-wide events and advocacy initiatives. Programs/events that pertain to diversity, education, health & wellness activities, and even hall competitions. Some of the programs we are known for around campus are Movie-Nights on McClendon
Hill, Progressive Snack Night (where we serve 7 different types of food in each of the residence hall lounges), and even a Halloween Costume and Door Decorating contest. Advocacy wise, for instance,
we have cleared out parking spaces that are not being used so that the residents in the suites may be able to park closer to the halls rather than parking behind the Ehinger Center. Besides the programming
and advocacy, RHA also co-sponsors with other clubs/organizations by providing aid through money, volunteers, and advertising. We deal with student concerns that pertain to construction being too loud, buying supplies for facilities to better clean the halls and restrooms or supplies for the dorms such
as coaches, chairs, microwaves. etc.
